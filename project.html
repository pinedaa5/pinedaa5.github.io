Botnets

Abstract: A botnet is a network of compromised devices controlled remotely by cybercriminals. These devices are used for attacks such as DDoS, spam campaigns, and distributing other malware. Botnets often use peer-to-peer communication to remain active and difficult to dismantle. Defense involves keeping systems updated, changing default passwords, and monitoring for suspicious network activity.

Malware Detection & Prevention

Abstract: Malware detection and prevention combine technology, policy, and user education to reduce risks. Traditional antivirus tools rely on known signatures, while modern systems use behavioral analytics and machine learning to detect anomalies. Organizations are encouraged to apply layered defenses including regular patching, endpoint monitoring, and user awareness training.
