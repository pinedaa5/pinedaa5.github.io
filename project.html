Ransomware

Abstract: Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts a victim’s files or systems and demands payment (a ransom) to restore access. Attackers often exploit phishing emails, insecure remote services, or outdated software. Once inside, ransomware spreads across networks, encrypting drives and backups. The impact includes technical disruption, financial loss, and reputational damage.

URL: Symantec Threat Intelligence

Author: Broadcom / Symantec Threat Intelligence Blog

Modern ransomware evolves constantly — changing encryption methods, delivery tactics, and extortion methods (e.g., threatening to leak stolen data). Preventive measures include system patching, offline backups, network segmentation, and strong authentication. Employee training and incident response planning are critical for minimizing damage.

Botnets

Abstract: A botnet is a network of compromised computers controlled remotely by attackers (botmasters). Devices become infected through phishing, vulnerabilities, or malicious downloads. Botnets are used for large-scale attacks such as DDoS, spam campaigns, and spreading other malware.

URLs: Kaspersky Research, Malwarebytes Labs

Authors: Kaspersky Security Research & Malwarebytes Labs

Botnets adapt by hiding control servers and using encrypted communications, making them resilient. They target businesses, home users, IoT devices, and servers. Defenses include patching, disabling unnecessary services, changing default passwords, and monitoring for unusual traffic. Law enforcement sometimes dismantles botnets by seizing command infrastructure.

Malware Detection & Prevention

Abstract: Malware detection and prevention combine technology, policies, and user education to reduce risks. Prevention includes patching, strong authentication, secure system configurations, and backups. Detection methods range from signature-based scanning to behavior-based analytics, anomaly detection, and threat intelligence integration.

URLs: CISA / US-CERT, NIST IR 7621

Authors: CISA / US-CERT, NIST

NIST guidelines emphasize layered security: prevention, detection, and response. This includes asset inventory, least-privilege access, secure baselines, and user awareness training. Organizations must prepare with monitoring tools, logging, and incident response strategies to quickly recover from attacks.
